The Core Concept: "Semantic Mixing"
Current audio tools (DAWs) work on frequencies (EQ, Volume). The tool you want needs to work on semantics (Meaning, Vibe). You shouldn't have to "filter out the low end"; you should be able to say, "Remove the car engine, keep the dialogue."
The UI Design: "CineWeave"
1. The "Source Miner" (Input & Separation)
This replaces the traditional file import. You drag in a movie file (e.g., Interstellar).
• Visual Source Separation (The "Meta" Magic): Instead of scrubbing through a waveform, you see the video player. You click on the actor's face, and SAM Audio instantly extracts only their voice. It removes the Hans Zimmer score and the sound effects automatically.
• Text Query: If the visual is messy, you type: "Isolate the female whisper, remove the rain noise." (Powered by AudioSep).
• Result: You get a pristine, dry vocal "stem" that you can layer over any music without clashing keys or muddy background noise.
2. The "Script Timeline" (Editing)
Waveforms are abstract; stories are textual.
• Transcription View: The timeline isn't a squiggly line; it’s the script of the scene (generated by OpenAI Whisper).
• Edit by Text: You highlight the sentence "Do not go gentle into that good night" and drag it onto your music track. The audio moves with the text.
• Keyword Search: You type "Hope" or "Melancholy," and the tool scans your entire movie library for quotes with that sentiment (using text embeddings).
3. The "Vibe Weaver" (Mixing)
This handles the "Cabincats" style of blending grand scores with spoken word.
• Smart Ducking: You don't draw volume automation curves. You drop the dialogue onto the music, and the tool uses Semantic Sidechaining. It doesn't just lower the volume; it carves out the specific frequencies of the voice from the music track, ensuring the speech is crystal clear while the bass rattles on.
• Key & Sentiment Lock: You upload a grand score. The tool analyzes its musical key (e.g., C Minor) and suggests dialogue clips that have a compatible pitch or emotional tone (e.g., "Somber," "Urgent").
4. The "Reference Decoder" (Analysis)
To truly "listen and analyze" like the artist Cabincats:
• Structure Mapping: You drag a Cabincats Roman Numeral mix into a "Reference" slot.
• Ghost Arrangement: The AI analyzes the energy flow: [0:00 Intro Drone] → [0:45 Dialogue Enter] → [1:20 Beat Drop]. It creates an empty template on your timeline, guiding you on where to place your own samples to match that specific narrative arc.
How to Build This (Tech Stack)
If you were to build a prototype today, this is the stack:
• Separation: facebook/sam-audio (Visual/Prompt separation) or audio-agi/AudioSep.
• Transcription: openai/whisper-large-v3 (for the script view).
• Vibe/Tagging: laion/CLAP (Contrastive Language-Audio Pretraining) to tag audio with moods.
• Analysis: librosa (Python) for detecting the Key and BPM of the music samples.
This workflow shifts the creative process from "engineering" (cleaning noise, matching levels) to "directing" (choosing the cast, setting the scene), which is exactly how narrative mixes should be made.
