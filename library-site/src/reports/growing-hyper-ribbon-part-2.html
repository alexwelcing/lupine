This is Part 2 of a two-part series. Part 1 — the mathematical framework develops the geometry, the regulator, and the catalog as a conjecture-grade scaffold. Part 2 reports what happened when that scaffold met real data.

Section 1What Changed: The Cross-Paradigm Reframing

Part 1's central empirical claim was that gold "escapes" the hyper-ribbon when foundation MLIPs are added, with its participation ratio jumping 1.02 → 1.67 under MACE and reproducing on CHGNet (1.58). Orb-v3 was listed Pending. The Round-2 implementation closes that gap — and in doing so sharpens what "escape" means.

◆ The key insight Au does not escape in the sense of being inconsistent across MLIPs. All three MLIPs agree on Au's error direction to within cosine 0.97–0.99. The "escape" is that Au's MLIP errors point in a different direction than its classical-potential errors — the foundation MLIPs form a distinct cluster from the 559 classical potentials. This is a cross-paradigm escape, not a cross-MLIP escape.

This distinction matters for the catalog design. A cross-MLIP escape would be model noise to average away. A cross-paradigm escape is a real, structured, correctable displacement — exactly the kind of regularity the Lupine regulator is built to detect and overlay. It also explains why Au is simultaneously the most self-consistent element across MLIPs and the most displaced from the classical ribbon: relativistic 5d/6s physics is reproducibly mis-handled by every PBE-trained foundation model in the same way.

Section 4Incremental SVD: The Computational Core

Real-time mid-run regulation needs the eigenbasis updated without recomputing from scratch. The IncrementalSVD class implements the Brand (2002) update at O(q²) per observation:

\mu_{n+1} = \mu_n + \frac{x_{n+1}-\mu_n}{n+1}, \qquad \tilde x = x_{n+1}-\mu_{n+1}, \qquad c = U_n^{\!\top}\tilde x, \quad p = \tilde x - U_n c

Form K=\begin{bmatrix}\operatorname{diag}\Sigma_n & c\\ 0 & \lVert p\rVert\end{bmatrix}, take its SVD U_K\Sigma_K V_K^{\!\top}, and update U_{n+1}=[\,U_n,\ p/\lVert p\rVert\,]\,U_K, \Sigma_{n+1}=\Sigma_K, truncated to the top q components.

Validation: the incremental result matches a full batch SVD within <1% relative error on the test data — confirming the hot path is numerically sound, not just fast.
