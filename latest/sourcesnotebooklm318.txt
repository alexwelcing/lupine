Data Quantity and Quality
	
Large-scale Materials & Molecular Datasets
	
Scarcity of high-fidelity experimental/DFT data; fragmented silos (crystals vs. proteins); inconsistent labels across datasets; proprietary barriers.
	
Consolidate resources (ZINC3D, PubChem3D, SAIR) using unified parameters (PBE functional, 500 eV cutoff). Leverage multi-task training (DPA-2) to merge datasets with heterogeneous labels.
	
Establish open datasets (JTH, SAIR with 5.2M structures); curate metallic alloy and drug-molecule datasets; develop NLP tools (MaterialsBERT) for literature data extraction.
	
Expand datasets to include transition-state structures, high-temperature sampling, and f-block elements; implement uncertainty-aware sampling and periodic retraining.
	
Achieve a universal Large Atomic Model (LAM) covering the entire periodic table; establish autonomous discovery flywheels and discovery-attribution marketplaces.
	
Industry: Provides project-specific SAR and experimental data. Academia: Curation of high-quality DFT/simulation datasets. National Laboratories: Maintenance of standardized FAIR repositories (NOMAD, Materials Project, NIST).

Data Quantity and Quality
	
Machine Learning Interatomic Potentials (ML-IAPs)
	
High computational cost of DFT; limited generalizability of empirical potentials; difficulty characterizing short-range order and phase transitions.
	
Develop ML-IAPs (NEP, DP, GAP, MACE) using flexible functional forms to achieve DFT-level accuracy with linear O(N) scaling. Use active learning (DP-GEN) to avoid redundant data.
	
Establish stable implementations on heterogeneous hardware (CPU/GPU/DCU); produce extensive first-principles data for DPA-1/DPA-2; validate against point defect energetics.
	
Integrate hybrid-functional precision via delta learning; automate gap identification in chemical space; refine potentials using 'sloppy-models' theory to identify error manifolds.
	
Achieve predictive accuracy <1 meV/atom across the entire periodic table; enable 100-million atom simulations for industrial applications (HEAs, batteries).
	
Industry: Hardware optimization (GPU acceleration). Academia: Algorithm development (equivariant architectures, neuroevolution). National Laboratories: Provision of massive first-principles reference data.

Data Quantity and Quality
	
Data Management & Provenance
	
Lack of data standards; reproducibility crisis; withholding of negative results; IP risks in multi-institution collaboration.
	
Implement blockchain-based mechanisms for provenance and supply-chain traceability. Define community metrics and standard metadata schemas (material, potential, conditions).
	
Define provenance best practices; implement decentralized storage (DeScAI S1) and hash-based versioning; establish scientific benefits of reporting negative results.
	
Coupling blockchain with federated learning; implement on-chain discovery attribution; develop AI workflows to incorporate negative data.
	
Global self-organizing manufacturing ecosystems with embedded lifecycle traceability; full interoperability between international repositories.
	
Industry: Ensure secure supply chains. Academia: Change research culture to value negative results. National Laboratories: Oversee governance standards and metadata frameworks.

Platforms and Infrastructure
	
Open-Source & Unified Software Ecosystems
	
Fragmented software landscape; commercial licensing costs; lack of standardization; "bare assets" with opaque interfaces.
	
Build modular, open-source architectures (ABACUS, OpenDFT, DeePMD-kit) supporting multi-backends (PyTorch, JAX). Turn assets into "Science-as-a-Service" via the Bohrium substrate.
	
Achieve VASP-compatibility in open-source codes; implement modular "Lego-like" assembly for code; standardize subsystems (Reading, Computing, Experiment).
	
Integrate differentiable physics engines for gradient-based learning; implement cloud-edge architectures to reduce real-time analysis latency.
	
A unified computational engine for the AI for Science paradigm; global end-to-end infrastructure unifying experimental, computational, and literature data.
	
Industry: Provide scalable cloud resources (AWS, Azure). Academia: Software design and modular algorithm development. National Laboratories: Infrastructure for massive parallelization (MPI/OpenMP) and exascale-ready code.

Platforms and Infrastructure
	
High-Performance Computing & Visualization
	
Computational complexity of QM methods; CPU-to-GPU bottlenecks; desktop-bound visualization software with high memory overhead.
	
Implement GPU-native simulators (GPUMD, Isaac Lab) and WebGPU-powered visualization (ATLAS View). Optimize software-hardware co-design for E-class systems.
	
Fit execution time on supercomputers with <20% error using performance models (DP-perf); render 10M+ atoms at 60fps in web browsers.
	
Refine models to include network topology and bandwidth contention; scale to 50M+ atom interactive visualization.
	
Enable simulations of 10 billion atoms with nanoseconds-per-day performance on emerging architectures; real-time high-fidelity 3D generation.
	
Industry: Software engineering and GPU kernel optimization (NVIDIA, Microsoft). Academia: Theoretical performance modeling. National Laboratories: Maintaining top-tier supercomputers (Fugaku, Summit).

AI for Specific Applications
	
Autonomous Discovery & Robotics
	
Time-consuming trial-and-error; high cost of lab automation; vast hypothesis space.
	
Create iterative closed-loop systems (Self-Driving Laboratories) driven by predictive modeling, active learning, and automated synthesis.
	
Demonstrate remote-controlled SDLs; pilot agentic evaluators (Coscientist) for specific chemical reactions; deploy mobile robots sharing human equipment.
	
Implement closed-loop Bayesian optimization for complex compositional spaces; integrate physics-informed digital twins to guide experiment selection.
	
Fully autonomous discovery of clean energy materials with 75% reduction in discovery time; achieve the 'Nobel-Turing Test' for paradigm-shifting discovery.
	
Industry: Robotic hardware manufacturing. Academia: Designing reasoning engines and active learning algorithms. National Laboratories: Hosting centralized robotic facilities and validation benchmarks.

AI for Specific Applications
	
Catalysis & Energy Materials
	
Complexity of electrochemical interfaces; sluggish kinetics in batteries; difficulty modeling non-adiabatic dynamics.
	
Combine AI-assisted methods with implicit solvation models and big data platforms (DigCat). Use MLPs to replicate QM accuracy for interfacial instabilities.
	
Launch platforms (DigCat 3.0) with experimental data points; benchmark MLPs for Li-ion transport; automate TS search for simple catalysts.
	
Develop on-the-fly microkinetic modeling; optimize quinary alloy catalysts for carbon neutrality; simulate radiation damage in fusion reactor materials.
	
Radical reduction in catalyst development costs; design of materials for carbon capture with DFT accuracy at O(N) cost.
	
Industry: Provision of performance metrics and industrial performance data. Academia: Research on reaction mechanisms and nuclear quantum effects. National Laboratories: Development of standardized benchmarks and nuclear environment testing.

AI for Specific Applications
	
Structure-Based Drug Design (SBDD)
	
Achieving kinase selectivity; scarcity of resolved protein structures; rigid docking limitations.
	
Integrate deep learning (AlphaFold, ESMFold) into structure-aware pipelines. Transition to geometry-informed architectures (GNNs, Diffusion) for pose prediction.
	
Achieve SOTA results on QM9/GEOM-DRUG; deploy binding-site identification tools (EquiPocket); validate multi-objective optimization (CK2/CLK3).
	
Refine diffusion-based 3D coordinate generators; integrate co-folding models; demonstrate high-accuracy protein pocket prediction.
	
Enable general-purpose foundation models for automated drug design; robust flexibility modeling for dynamic and cryptic pockets.
	
Industry: Integration of models into pharmaceutical R&D pipelines. Academia: Publishing benchmarks for protein-ligand tasks. National Laboratories: Experimental validation of AI-designed crystals/molecules.

Collaboration and Education
	
Interdisciplinary Education & Workforce
	
Shortage of "centaur scientists" (AI + Physical Science); training/UX gating factors; fragmentation of research disciplines.
	
Establish interdisciplinary PhD pathways; reform curricula (Manufacturing Extension Partnership); build AI agents (MatSciAgent) with natural language interfaces.
	
Launch interdisciplinary courses; develop grammar-constrained LLM assistants for script generation; document discrete digitalization use cases.
	
Scale multi-university initiatives to create science-ready LLMs; formalize "Science of AI" as a distinct discipline.
	
Establish robust "national power" industry strategies; normalize cross-sector R&D labs; global leadership in AI-driven materials discovery.
	
Industry: Funding fellowships and co-investing in research centers. Academia: Curriculum development and interdisciplinary training. National Laboratories: Facilitating international collaboration and open-access toolkits.

Open Science & Community Initiatives
	
Model opacity; lack of interpretability; fragmented software ecosystem; misaligned incentives.
	
Launch community-wide initiatives (OpenLAM); promote "Science-as-a-Service"; implement cryptoeconomic incentives (Quadratic Funding) for research.
	
Launch OpenLAM Initiative; provide tutorial notebooks (Bohrium); implement Sybil-hard identity for under-resourced labs.
	
Establish datasets with domain impact (ImageNet-style) for atomic modeling; implement automated agent-assisted capability onboarding.
	
Shift to computational epistemology where human-agent teams invent knowledge; transformation of the scientific social contract into a trust-minimized ecology.
	
Industry: Sponsoring community development and providing capital for prediction markets. Academia: Participating in pluralistic peer review and governance. National Laboratories: Data stewardship and providing public ledgers as scientific memory.
