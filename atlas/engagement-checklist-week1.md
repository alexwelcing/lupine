# Week 1 Engagement Checklist
## Starting the Representation Team

**Goal:** Engage US legal counsel and begin China counsel search  
**Timeline:** March 15-22, 2026  
**Success criteria:** Signed engagement letter with US counsel, initial calls with 2-3 China counsel candidates

---

## Monday: US Legal Counsel

### Morning: Research

**Target firms:**
- Wilson Sonsini Goodrich & Rosati (WSGR)
- Fenwick & West
- Cooley LLP
- Gunderson Dettmer
- Orrick

**Selection criteria:**
- Early-stage venture experience ($500K-$5M rounds)
- Materials/deep tech sector experience
- Speed (will they move fast for a pre-revenue company?)
- Personality fit (do they believe in you?)

### Afternoon: Outreach

**Warm intro route:**
- Check LinkedIn for mutual connections to partners
- Ask other founders for referrals
- Check if any investors you know have relationships

**Cold outreach template:**

> Subject: Pre-revenue computational materials company — considering strategic options
>
> [Partner name],
>
> I'm the founder of ATLAS, a computational materials science platform (molecular dynamics + ML potentials). We have working technology and are preparing for a strategic financing round this summer.
>
> The situation is somewhat complex — we have interest from both US strategic investors and international strategic investors, and we're working through the structure and implications.
>
> I'm looking for counsel who can:
> - Move quickly (this is time-sensitive)
> - Handle Delaware C-Corp formation and venture financing
> - Think through complex cross-border considerations
> - Has experience with "hot" technical companies
>
> Are you taking on new early-stage clients? If not, can you recommend someone at the firm who is?
>
> [Your name]

### Evening: Follow-up

- Track responses
- Schedule intro calls for Tuesday/Wednesday

---

## Tuesday: Initial US Counsel Calls

### Call agenda (30 min each):

1. **Your story** (5 min)
   - ATLAS technology
   - Current status
   - Summer timeline

2. **The situation** (5 min)
   - US interest
   - China interest
   - Need for dual preparation

3. **Their experience** (5 min)
   - Similar situations?
   - Speed of execution?
   - Fee structure?

4. **Next steps** (5 min)
   - Engagement letter timeline
   - Immediate needs
   - Any conflicts?

### Red flags:
- Pushing you hard toward US option (should present options neutrally)
- Dismissive of China complexity (shows inexperience)
- Can't move fast (summer is 12 weeks away)
- Too expensive for your stage (expect $50-100K total, not $300K)

### Green flags:
- Asks good questions about technology
- Has seen cross-border situations before
- Proposes pragmatic approach
- Energized by the challenge

### Evening: Decision

- Select primary counsel
- Request engagement letter
- Notify runners-up

## Thursday: China Counsel Outreach

### Warm intro route (preferred):

Ask your network:
- Anyone who has done China deals?
- Anyone who has set up WFOE?
- Anyone at multinational with China legal team?

### Cold outreach template:

> Subject: Foreign founder establishing WFOE — venture financing planned
>
> [Partner name],
>
> I'm a US founder building ATLAS, a computational materials science software platform. I have technical traction and am preparing for venture financing this summer with Chinese strategic investors.
>
> I am planning to establish a WFOE and relocate to China to build the company. I need counsel who can:
>
> - Guide WFOE formation and registered capital structure
> - Navigate foreign founder work permit process
> - Review venture term sheets (likely from state-affiliated funds)
> - Protect founder interests in cross-border structure
>
> Do you have experience with foreign founder setups? If so, can we schedule a brief call to discuss?
>
> [Your name]

### Note on language:

Most top-tier China firm partners speak excellent English. Don't worry about Mandarin for initial outreach.

### Evening: Track responses

- Expect 24-48 hour response time (China timezone)
- Schedule calls for next week

## Resource: Questions for China Counsel Screening

### Experience:
1. How many foreign founder WFOE setups have you done in the last 2 years?
2. What percentage of your practice is venture capital?
3. Have you worked with [target investors, if known]?
4. Do you have dual-qualified lawyers on the team?

### Process:
5. Timeline for WFOE formation?
6. What are the biggest mistakes foreign founders make?
7. How do you handle work permit complexities?
8. What's your fee structure for this type of engagement?

### Protection:
9. How do you protect foreign founders in Chinese venture deals?
10. What are the red flags in China term sheets?
11. How do you handle legal representative liability?
12. What happens if things go wrong?

### Cultural:
13. How do you communicate with foreign clients? (English fluency?)
14. Will I work directly with a partner or junior associates?
15. Do you have relationships with the investors I'm targeting?

## Blockers to Watch For

| Blocker | Mitigation |
|---------|------------|
| US counsel too slow | Have backup candidates, be clear about timeline |
| China counsel unresponsive | Try multiple firms, use warm intros |
| Passport delayed | Check status weekly, prepare for emergency appointment |
| Fees too high | Negotiate cap, payment schedule, or explore alternative firms |
| Conflicts of interest | Ask upfront, get confirmation in writing |
