## Part 2: The Signal Strategy

Chinese investors are skeptical of Americans because too many have played them. You need to send **unambiguous signals of commitment** from day one.

### Tier 1 Signals (Essential)

**1. Family relocation plan**
- Specific school identified and contacted
- Spouse employment solution (or resignation plan)
- Housing research completed
- Timeline: "We can move within 60 days of funding"

**2. Entity structure commitment**
- WFOE (Wholly Foreign-Owned Enterprise) preferred by you
- Not "we'll see" — "I want to incorporate as [City] ATLAS Technology Co., Ltd."
- Registered capital commitment (even if minimal initially)

**3. Technology localization plan**
- Core R&D in China
- Chinese employees with full codebase access
- Chinese cloud infrastructure (Alibaba Cloud, Tencent Cloud)
- Chinese Git server (Gitee, not GitHub for sensitive repos)

**4. Personal commitment statement**
- "I am relocating to China to build this company"
- "My family is coming with me"
- "I am learning Mandarin" (even if just basics — shows intent)

### Tier 2 Signals (Differentiating)

**5. Government relationship cultivation**
- Identify specific local government targets (Hefei, Shenzhen, Shanghai)
- Understand their "guidance fund" priorities
- Prepare to align with local industrial policy

**6. Academic partnerships**
- Identify CAS institute partners (ICCAS, etc.)
- Prepare joint research proposals
- Show you understand Chinese academic ecosystem

**7. Long-term timeline**
- "5-year commitment minimum"
- "Building a Chinese team that can outlast me"
- "Training the next generation of Chinese computational materials scientists"

### What NOT to Say

❌ "I'm weighing options between US and China"  
❌ "I want to keep the IP in Singapore/US"  
❌ "My family might stay in the US initially"  
❌ "I'll commute between countries"  
❌ "What's the fastest path to exit/IPO?"

These are deal-killers. They indicate you're not serious.

## Part 3: Location Strategy

Where you build matters enormously in China.

### Option A: Hefei (合肥)

**Why it works:**
- China "science city" — USTC, CAS institutes, quantum/materals focus
- Lower cost than coastal cities
- Aggressive local government incentives
- Less international scrutiny than Beijing/Shanghai

**Challenges:**
- Mandarin essential (limited expat community)
- Smaller international school options
- Less "glamorous" for international investors

**Best for:** Pure R&D focus, deep government integration, cost efficiency

### Option B: Shenzhen (深圳)

**Why it works:**
- China's Silicon Valley — tech ecosystem, talent pool
- Close to Hong Kong (escape hatch, international access)
- Entrepreneurial culture
- Strong local government support (famous for efficiency)

**Challenges:**
- Expensive (for China)
- Housing crisis
- Less academic/government focus than Hefei

**Best for:** Speed, startup culture, international connectivity

### Option C: Shanghai (上海)

**Why it works:**
- International city, best schools for family
- Strong financial ecosystem
- Major expat community
- Prestige location

**Challenges:**
- Most expensive
- Less favorable government incentives (they don't need you)
- More bureaucratic

**Best for:** Family comfort, international optics, exit planning

### Recommendation

**Primary:** Hefei or Shenzhen for operations  
**Secondary:** Shanghai satellite if family needs international community  
**Timeline:** Start in one, expand to others as you grow

### Children Considerations

**School options:**
- International schools (Shanghai American School, etc.) — $30-50K/year, English, easy transition
- Local private schools — Mandarin immersion, cheaper, harder transition
- Local public schools — Full immersion, very hard transition

**Age matters:**
- Under 8: Adapt quickly, Mandarin acquisition natural
- 8-12: Moderate adaptation, social challenges
- 13+: Very difficult, consider staying US with one parent (not recommended for family)

Before committing to raise:

- [ ] School tours and applications
- [ ] Housing research
- [ ] Spouse meets potential community
- [ ] Honest family discussion about concerns

**If family is not 100% on board, do not proceed.** A founder with family problems cannot build effectively.

## Part 5: Entity Structure (China)

### WFOE Structure (Recommended)

```
ATLAS Technology Co., Ltd. (WFOE)
├── Location: Hefei/Shenzhen/Shanghai
├── Business scope: Software development, technical consulting, import/export
├── Registered capital: RMB 1-10M (can be contributed over time)
├── Structure: Foreign-owned, you as legal representative
└── Timeline: 2-3 months to establish
```

### Key Roles

**Legal Representative (法定代表人):**
- You (as founder)
- Personal liability for company actions
- Cannot leave China easily if company has debts/issues
- **This is serious** — understand the liability

**Supervisor (监事):**
- Spouse or trusted advisor
- Checks and balances on legal representative
- Cannot be same person as legal representative

**Key points:**
- You will need a Chinese address (office lease)
- You will need a Chinese bank account
- You will need to file monthly tax returns (accountant essential)
- Annual audit required

### Alternative: Joint Venture

**If government fund insists:**
- Local government guidance fund as minority partner
- You maintain operational control
- Better access to local resources
- More complex governance

**Only consider if:**
- It's the only path to funding
- You negotiate strong control provisions
- You have excellent legal counsel

## Part 6: The Visa Path

### Step 1: Work Permit (工作许可证)

**Requirements:**
- Bachelor's degree (yours is fine)
- 2+ years relevant experience
- Clean criminal record (FBI background check)
- Health check
- Employer sponsorship (your WFOE, once established)

**Timeline:** 4-6 weeks after WFOE established

**Duration:** 1-2 years initially, renewable

### Step 2: Residence Permit (居留许可)

**Requirements:**
- Work permit
- Housing registration
- Application at local PSB (Public Security Bureau)

**Timeline:** 2-4 weeks

**Duration:** Matches work permit

### Step 3: Family Visas

**Spouse/Children:**
- S1 visa (for stays >180 days)
- Convert to residence permit on arrival
- Your work permit is prerequisite

**Timeline:** Parallel to yours

### The "Green Card" (Permanent Residence)

**Options:**
- Employment-based: 4+ years continuous work, high salary/tax contribution
- Investment-based: Significant investment (RMB 2M+)
- Talent-based: Recognized expert (you might qualify after success)

**Reality:** Very difficult for foreigners, especially Americans. Plan on annual renewals for 5+ years.

## Part 9: Risk Management

### Personal Safety

**Low probability, but plan:**
- Maintain US passport (do not renounce citizenship)
- Keep US bank accounts accessible
- Maintain relationships with US embassy/consulate
- Know exit routes (Hong Kong still accessible, though harder)
- Consider: Will your family ever be "hostage" to your good behavior? (paranoid but real in extreme cases)

### Business Risk

**Technology transfer requirements:**
- You will be expected to train Chinese employees on full stack
- Your IP will be in China, subject to Chinese law
- "Forced technology transfer" is less explicit now, but still real via market access

**Mitigation:**
- Maintain personal copies of core IP (not in China)
- Build modular architecture (can extract core if needed)
- Keep Singapore entity as backup (though this signals lack of commitment)

### Geopolitical Risk

**US sanctions escalation:**
- You could be personally sanctioned (unlikely for materials software, possible for defense applications)
- Your company could be added to entity list
- This would kill any future US relationships

**China internal politics:**
- Regulatory changes (cybersecurity, data localization)
- Anti-foreign sentiment fluctuations
- Your industry could fall out of favor

**Mitigation:**
- Stay in "friendly" industries (energy, batteries) vs. "sensitive" (military, semiconductors)
- Build government relationships (protects you)
- Keep head down, deliver value
