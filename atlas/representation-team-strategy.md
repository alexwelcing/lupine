# ATLAS Representation Team Strategy
## Building Credibility for a Competitive Relocation

**Core Insight:** You are not a desperate founder begging for Chinese capital. You are a valuable asset that the US wants to keep and China must win. This repositioning changes everything.

---

## Executive Summary

Your representation team serves two functions:
1. **US Function:** Establish credibility, create competitive tension, provide off-ramp if China fails
2. **China Function:** Navigate complexity, signal seriousness, ensure deal protects your interests

**The narrative:** "US investors are actively courting me to stay. I am open to China, but the burden of winning me is on Chinese investors — through terms, support, and a genuine home for my family."

---

## Part 1: The Pricing of "Winning"

### Why China Must Pay More

| Factor | US Path | China Path | Premium Justification |
|--------|---------|------------|----------------------|
| **Geographic risk** | Familiar home market | Foreign jurisdiction | Higher valuation for uncertainty |
| **Career risk** | Maintain US optionality | Close US doors | Irreversibility premium |
| **Execution complexity** | Standard Delaware C-Corp | WFOE + work permits + relocation | Higher valuation for friction |
| **Family displacement** | Stay in US ecosystem | Relocate to China | Quality-of-life premium |
| **Political risk** | Stable (relatively) | Geopolitical uncertainty | Risk-adjusted return |
| **Liquidity risk** | Clear IPO/M&A path | China exit uncertain | Illiquidity premium |

**Translation:** A US offer at $10M pre-money is equivalent to a China offer at $15-20M pre-money when you account for total cost of relocation and risk.

### The Discovery Value

You mentioned "invaluable discovery" — this is real. You are discovering:
- Whether Chinese investors will compete seriously for US technical talent
- What the "winning premium" actually is
- Whether they understand the burden they're asking you to bear

**This discovery is worth something.** Don't give it away for free.

## Part 3: The Signaling Strategy

### Week 1-2: Establish US Baseline

**Actions:**
- Engage US legal counsel
- Incorporate Delaware C-Corp (signals US is real option)
- Engage US advisory board member
- Begin soft outreach to US targets

**Signal to China:** "I have credible US option. I am listening to China, but you must compete."

**Signal to US:** "I am considering my options seriously."

### Week 3-4: Open China Channel

**Actions:**
- Engage China legal counsel
- Begin China relationship cultivation
- Mention US interest in China conversations ("I have US term sheet discussions")
- Ask China investors: "What does winning look like for you?"

**Signal to China:** "I am serious, but I'm not desperate. What's your offer?"

**Signal to US:** "China is interested. Are you going to make this competitive?"

### Month 2: Develop Both Tracks

**Actions:**
- US: Formal pitch meetings, term sheet discussions
- China: Formal pitch meetings, term sheet discussions
- Maintain strict compartmentalization
- Let both sides know the other exists (but nothing else)

**Critical:** US advisory board member makes public statement or introduction that validates US interest.

### Month 3: The Reveal

**To China investors:**
> "I have a credible US offer at [valuation/terms]. I am genuinely considering China because [market/impact/personal reasons]. But I need you to understand what I am giving up: [list career/family/geographic costs]. For me to choose China, the offer needs to reflect that this is a win for you, not just a market-rate deal."

**To US investors (if they push):**
> "I have a competitive situation. I am evaluating both options. My family and I are open to China if the opportunity is compelling enough. I need to see your best offer by [date]."

## Part 4: The "Great Home" Negotiation

### What to Ask For (Beyond Valuation)

Chinese investors can offer things US investors cannot. These have real value:

#### Family Support Package

| Item | Value | How to Ask |
|------|-------|------------|
| International school tuition | $30-50K/year | "Family support through first 3 years" |
| Housing stipend | $3-8K/month | "Relocation assistance for family" |
| Spouse employment support | Networking/job placement | "Introductions for spouse career" |
| Healthcare coverage | $10-20K/year | "Comprehensive family insurance" |
| Home leave flights | $5K/year | "Annual family travel to US" |
| Mandarin tutoring | $5-10K/year | "Language support for integration" |

**Frame as:** "My family's success is my success. I need support to ensure they're thriving, not just surviving."

#### Quality of Life Guarantees

- **Air quality:** Commitment to air purifiers, indoor environments
- **Housing location:** Specific compounds known for expat families
- **Community:** Introductions to other expat families
- **Emergency support:** What happens if things go wrong

#### Career Protection

- **Board seat preservation:** You maintain control despite being foreign founder
- **Exit protection:** What happens if you need to leave China
- **US relationship maintenance:** Permission to maintain minimal US advisory roles
- **IP protection:** What you can take with you if you leave

### The Conversation

> "I am prepared to commit to China — to relocate, to build the team there, to localize the technology. This is a 5-10 year commitment with significant personal and career costs. My US option is [X]. For me to choose China, I need:
> 
> 1. **Valuation premium:** [Y]% above US offer to account for risk and illiquidity
> 2. **Family support:** [specific items] for [duration]
> 3. **Operational control:** [specific governance provisions]
> 4. **Quality of life:** [specific commitments]
> 
> I want to build ATLAS in China. But I need China to want me enough to make this work for my family and me."

## Part 6: The "Winning" Premium Framework

### Valuation Math

Assume US offer is $10M pre-money on $2M raise (20% dilution).

**China equivalent should be:**

| Adjustment | Factor | China Valuation |
|------------|--------|-----------------|
| Base US offer | 1.0x | $10M |
| Geographic risk premium | +15% | $11.5M |
| Career irreversibility premium | +20% | $13.8M |
| Execution complexity premium | +10% | $15.2M |
| Family displacement premium | +15% | $17.5M |
| Liquidity risk premium | +10% | $19.3M |
| **Total premium** | **~90%** | **~$19M** |

**Your ask:** $18-20M pre-money for China, or equivalent non-valuation terms.

### Non-Valuation Equivalents

If they won't meet valuation premium, extract value elsewhere:

| Valuation Gap | Non-Valuation Equivalent |
|---------------|--------------------------|
| $2M gap | Family support package for 3 years |
| $3M gap | Founder liquidity (secondary) at close |
| $4M gap | Board control + anti-dilution protection |
| $5M gap | Guaranteed minimum exit commitment |

**With yourself:**
- [ ] Am I genuinely open to either outcome?
- [ ] What is my walk-away price from China?
- [ ] What is my walk-away price from US?
- [ ] What happens if neither meets my number?

**With your wife:**
- [ ] Does she understand this might become public?
- [ ] Is she prepared for potential US backlash ("traitor" narrative)?

- [ ] What is HER walk-away condition?

- [ ] US counsel: Are you comfortable representing me if I choose China?
- [ ] China counsel: Are you comfortable representing me if I choose US?
- [ ] Tax advisor: Can you model both scenarios?
