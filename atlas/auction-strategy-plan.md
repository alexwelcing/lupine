## Executive Summary

This is a different game than the "firewall" model. You are:
- **Weaponizing urgency:** National security timelines compress decision cycles

### Phase 2: Controlled Leakage (Week 2-3)

**Objective:** Each side knows the other is bidding, but not terms.

**US leak strategy:**
- Brief In-Q-Tel: "We've been approached by Chinese strategic capital"
- Mention in passing: "We're weighing our options given the geopolitical climate"
- Emphasize: "We want to build in the US, but need to see real commitment"

**China leak strategy:**
- Brief SDIC contact: "US defense innovation funds are very interested"
- Mention: "We prefer China market, but need competitive terms"
- Emphasize: "Full commitment to localization and domestic ecosystem"

**Critical constraint:** Never reveal actual numbers or terms to the other side. Only that "competition exists."

### Phase 3: Formal Process (Week 3-4)

**Objective:** Run parallel processes with synchronized timelines.

#### Process Design

```
Week 3:
  Monday:  Send term sheet request to both sides
  Tuesday: US side pitches to glim
  Wednesday: China side pitches to glim
  Thursday: glim presents to both (separate meetings)
  Friday:   Draft terms circulated

Week 4:
  Monday:  First round bids due
  Tuesday: Best and final offers
  Wednesday: Decision
  Thursday: Sign term sheet
  Friday:   Announce
```

**Key terms to negotiate:**
- Valuation (obviously)
- Board seats (keep control)
- Exclusivity period (minimize)
- Conditions precedent (know what can kill the deal)
- Government approval requirements (CFIUS? MOFCOM?)

### Phase 4: The Reveal (Week 4)

**When you choose:**

If **US** wins:
- Immediate press: "glim raises $XM from [strategic] to secure American materials science future"
- Subtext to China: You lost a strategic asset
- Follow-up: CFIUS filing, security clearance pathway for team

If **China** wins:
- Controlled announcement (less fanfare due to sensitivity)
- Subtext to US: Another tech asset lost to strategic competitor
- Follow-up: Relocation, localization plan, government relationship building

## 4. Valuation Maximization Tactics

### Tactic 1: The Strategic Denial Premium

**Pitch:** glim isn't just a company, it's a *chokepoint*. Whoever controls it denies the other side capabilities.

**US pitch angle:**
> "Every Chinese materials lab runs LAMMPS on Western tools. glim gives us the next generation. If we don't build it, they will. And then we lose visibility into their hypersonic materials program."

**China pitch angle:**
> "US software bans are coming. MATLAB, COMSOL, next is molecular dynamics. Without glim, China's semiconductor and battery programs depend on tools that can be shut off overnight."

### Tactic 2: The Team Anchor

**Your leverage:** You are the technical visionary. The team is you + key researchers.

**Execution:**
- Make clear you will only work with the winning bidder
- Emphasize personal commitment to chosen jurisdiction
- Hint at "other opportunities" if terms aren't right
- Never bluff — be prepared to walk

### Tactic 3: The Technology Window

**Artificial scarcity:** Set a hard deadline for decision.

> "We need to close by [date] to:
> - Hit SBIR submission deadline (if US)
> - Meet localization fund deployment window (if China)
> - Lock in key hires before they go to competitors
> - Secure GPU compute contracts before pricing increases"

**Real urgency + manufactured urgency = compressed negotiations**

### Tactic 4: The Milestone Ratchet

**Structure:** Valuation increases if they move faster.

| Timeline | Valuation | Rationale |
|----------|-----------|-----------|
| 2 weeks | $XM (target) | Speed premium |
| 4 weeks | $XM * 0.9 | Time cost |
| 6 weeks | $XM * 0.8 | Opportunity cost |
| 8 weeks | Walk | "We had to take other option" |

**Never say the walk number. Just let them feel it.**

### Tactic 5: The Strategic Co-Invest Constraint

**Leverage:** Each side wants strategic validation from the other.

**US offer:** "If In-Q-Tel leads, we can bring in Anduril and a top-tier VC"
**China offer:** "If SDIC leads, CATL and battery majors will follow"

**Reality:** You want one strategic + one financial. But use the combo as leverage.

## 7. Information Security During Auction

### Compartmentalization Protocol

**You need two separate negotiation tracks:**

| Aspect | US Track | China Track |
|--------|----------|-------------|
| Email | US Gmail / Proton | WeChat / QQ |
| Phone | US number | China number |
| Meetings | US time zone | China time zone |
| Docs | Google Drive | Tencent Docs / offline |
| Code demos | Clean US version | Clean China version |
| Team members | US-facing staff | China-facing staff |

**Golden rule:** Information crosses ONLY through you. No team member talks to both sides.

### Counter-Intelligence

**Assume:**
- US side will investigate your China contacts
- China side will investigate your US contacts
- Both will review your GitHub, social media, past employment

**Prep:**
- Audit all public profiles
- Document all past foreign contacts (for clearance if US)
- Ensure no active foreign government relationships
- Clean up any "problematic" past statements
