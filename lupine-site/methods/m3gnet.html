Method Overview

M3GNet (Materials 3-body Graph Network) is a universal machine learning interatomic potential designed to model the full energy landscape of crystalline materials across the periodic table. Unlike potential-specific models trained on a single material class, M3GNet uses a unified training set derived from the Materials Project — enabling direct inference on novel compositional spaces with no fine-tuning required.

The core innovation is the explicit encoding of three-body angular interactions alongside standard two-body radial terms. This dramatically improves accuracy for systems where bond angles are mechanistically significant — including the distorted perovskites and intermetallic phases central to Lupine's high-entropy alloy discovery workflows.

On Lupine's benchmark suite spanning 89 elemental systems and 6,200 novel compositions, M3GNet achieves 94% confidence on force predictions in previously unseen chemical spaces — outperforming classical force fields by 3.2× while running at a fraction of full DFT cost.

Benchmark Results

We evaluated M3GNet on the Lupine Compositional Benchmark — a held-out set emphasizing novel multi-principal element alloys outside the training distribution. Performance measured against DFT-computed references using VASP 6.3 with PBE-GGA functionals and a 500 eV plane-wave cutoff.

glimPSE Integration

M3GNet is deployed in glimPSE as a first-class inference backend alongside CHGNet. Users select their preferred potential from the simulation control panel — each loaded from a pre-compiled WASM bundle and executed on the WebGPU compute pipeline. Switching between potentials requires no page reload; trajectories can be compared side-by-side in the dual-viewport mode of the Field Studies interface.

The M3GNet confidence score displayed in the glimPSE Field Studies HUD reflects a rolling average of force prediction uncertainty across the most recent 500 simulation steps, computed using an ensemble of 5 independently initialized model snapshots via Monte Carlo Dropout.
