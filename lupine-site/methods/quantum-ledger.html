The Reproducibility Problem in ML-Driven MD

The publication of machine-learning molecular dynamics results faces a structural reproducibility crisis. Unlike classical force-field simulations with deterministic initial conditions, NNFF-driven trajectories carry an additional layer of non-reproducibility: model version, hardware floating-point behavior, batch parallelization order, and PRNG seed all affect the precise trajectory — even when the input structure and potential parameters appear identical.

Lupine's Quantum Ledger is our answer to this problem. It is a cryptographically-linked provenance layer embedded inside the glimPSE simulation engine that records every inference step of every simulation run — from force predictions and energy evaluations to the hardware context and model checkpoint hash used at runtime.

Every trajectory exported from glimPSE carries a Ledger Manifest: a compact JSON document that enables full bit-for-bit reproduction of any simulation, in any browser, on any hardware — given the same WASM bundle version and hardware floating-point standard.

Ledger Entry Schema

Each simulation step writes a single Ledger Entry to the in-memory append-only log. At trajectory export, these entries are serialized and embedded in the XDATCAR file header as a Base64-encoded JSON block.

Example Ledger Entry — Step 1,247
step_id1247
timestamp_ns1710432847293810432
potentialCHGNet@v0.3.3.sha256:a4f9c2
energy_eV-847.234192
max_force_eV_A0.04128
temperature_K2400.0
hardware_ctxWebGPU/Apple M3 Pro
entry_hashsha256:9e3f1a…

Each entry is chained to the previous via its SHA-256 hash, forming a tamper-evident linked list. Any modification to a historical step will invalidate every subsequent hash — making post-hoc trajectory manipulation detectable without requiring a trusted third party.

Quantum Ledger Synchronization

When a simulation completes in glimPSE, the Ledger Manifest is written to Lupine's distributed Ledger network — a content-addressed storage layer built on IPFS. This gives every Lupine simulation a permanent, globally-addressable proof of computation that can be cited in publications using a standard DOI-equivalent URI:

lupine://ledger/sha256:9e3f1a7c…

Reviewers and collaborators can independently verify a simulation's integrity by fetching the manifest and re-running the trajectory from its recorded initial conditions through the pinned WASM bundle version referenced in the manifest header.
