Executive Abstract

The shift from silicon-static to organic-fluid architecture represents the core objective of the glimPSE initiative. By leveraging biosynthetic neural nodes, we achieve data densities previously theoretical, housed within substrates that self-repair and evolve under systemic pressure.

I. Substrate Initialization

Initialization begins with the deposition of a synthetic mycelium lattice. Unlike traditional lithography, this protocol utilizes a growth-vectoring catalyst that aligns cellular growth with the desired computational pathways.

The resulting "web" acts as both data bus and energy conduit. The latency observed in organic nodes is mitigated by massive parallelism, effectively creating a 1:1 ratio between processing power and memory storage.
